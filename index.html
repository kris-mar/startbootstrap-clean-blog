Kristen Hanlon

System, Interaction, & Experience Designer

Whether it's a door labeled push when it's a pull or the betrayal of taking a bite out of an oatmeal cookie thinking it's chocolate chip, user experience is integral to our everyday lives. Everyone craves good design and my aim is to make the world a simpler and more intuitive place.

Hey there! I’m a designer currently based in Seattle. I love utilizing my background in STEM, fine arts, and marketing to create well-rounded experiences. I currently work for Microsoft as a Product Designer on the Photos App.

My story begins in bright tropical Miami with me in a lab coat. I started as a biomedical student, my entire life planned out to work in conservation genetics. I was working in a research laboratory when I was asked to make my first website for the Veterinarian Club at UCF. I begrudgingly agreed and found myself at Wordpress.com. The next 8 hours melted by and before I knew it, I fell in love! I was enamored with the colors, shapes, and mostly, the fact that good design can bring people together and feel pride in their involvement.

I continued my education in biology alongside art classes. I would spend my mornings in the studio and my evenings in the lab. I would go on to work with every kind of client I could. I designed for brands like AutoNation, UCF, and I even had a brief stint designing headstones. I was always searching for the perfect marriage between science and art; when I discovered UX, it felt like I found my soulmate.

In the past 3 years I’ve had the opportunity to work for small brands and big brands alike and gain experience in many facets of the UX field. My workflow includes heavy use of wireframes, prototypes, frontend development, and the Adobe creative suite.
